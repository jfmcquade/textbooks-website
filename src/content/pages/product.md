## Why this is possible now

## How it works

> _Is this for our three target audiences, or is it general for now? This should be a simple step-by-step process from the user's perspective, for the product we are working to build. (Maybe this doesn't work because we don't have a single target user.)_

> _How-it-works logic (TBD where this goes on the site, but good to know — this is currently for everyone, and shows the whole system):_

1. **Adding and PreTeXt-ifying textbooks** to integrate them into the library system: send us your Creative Commons–licensed textbook, and we'll convert it to PreTeXt and add it to the library. _(Lily's work, with the help of George's AI agents.)_
2. **Converting a library textbook into a variant of a "foundational" version:** Gabe is working on this — one foundational textbook, the other a variant of it (e.g. a Jamovi variant of "Intro to R with Stats"). "Intent tagging" is the identification of the differences between the foundational version and the variant. _(Steps 1 & 2 in prototype by ICOTS.)_
3. **Enabling anyone to create a new variant** based on an existing textbook.
4. **Enabling components of different textbooks to be combined** into an entirely new variant — down to the identified object (currently paragraph and above: assessments, sections, chapters, as shaped by usefulness). E.g. you could end up with multiple textbooks, each with its own set of assessment questions, targeted to the needs / interests / strengths / weaknesses of an individual student or group of students.
5. **A whole, coherent system** which enables things to be maintained, kept up to date, and continuously added to.

Goal: a fully functioning version (with steps 1–5) within a year. _(We should share this on the website.)_
